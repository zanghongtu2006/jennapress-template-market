In Jenna Press, official pages should stay small in number and stable in purpose. Home explains the project quickly, About explains the background, and Principles explains the rules.

The blog then becomes the expansion layer. Project articles explain why the framework exists and what it currently supports. Usage articles explain how to work with content, templates, and deployment.

That split keeps the homepage readable and the information architecture compact, even when the total amount of writing grows.
