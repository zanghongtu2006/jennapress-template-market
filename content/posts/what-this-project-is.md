Jenna Press is a static-first framework for small project websites that need a homepage, a few official pages, and a structured blog. It is not trying to become a general CMS, a headless platform, or a backend product.

The intended use is straightforward: keep content in markdown, keep templates in template directories, and keep framework rules predictable. When those boundaries stay visible, a small site can remain easy to publish and easy to evolve.

That is also why the project only keeps two blog categories by default: **Project** for understanding the framework itself, and **Usage** for learning how to work with it.
