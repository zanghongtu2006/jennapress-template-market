When adding a new page, first ask whether it belongs in the small set of stable official pages or whether it should really be a blog post. Jenna Press prefers fewer official pages and more topic-specific blog entries.

When adding a new post, choose one of the two existing categories unless there is a strong reason to expand the information architecture. The project uses categories to keep reading paths predictable, not to maximize taxonomy complexity.

In both cases, keep the front matter explicit, the slug stable, and the markdown body readable.
